HELEN KELLER



About

Helen Adams Keller,(born June 27,1880 & died June 1,1968),U.S Author and educator who was blind and deaf,Keller was deprived by illness of sight and hearing at the age of 19 months,and her speech development soon ceased as well. Eventually Keller learned to read and write in Braille.She wrote many books, including 'The story of My Life'(1902)

“Life is either a great adventure or nothing.”

- Helen Keller.

Here's a time line of Helen Keller's life:

  • 1880 - Born in Tuscumbia,Alabama

  • 1881 - At the age of 19 months lost sight and hearing as a result of meningitis

  • 1886 - Recognized by Alexander Graham Bell as being exceptionally bright

  • 1887 - Began instruction under Anne Sullivan

  • 1888 - Learned to read and write in Braille

  • 1900 - Enrolled in Radcliffe College as a regular student with the help of Anne Sullivan

  • 1903 - Published her first book 'The Story of My Life'

  • 1908 - Published The World I Live In

  • 1904 - First blind/deaf college graduate to graduate from Radcliffe College

  • 1915 - Helen Keller International(HKI) was founded

  • 1924 - Held many lectures and tours in the united states of America

  • 1946 - Helen visited over 35 different countries.she spoke on behalf of disabled people,and many governments opened schools for the blind and deaf because of her.

  • 1956 - First woman to receive an honorary degree from Harvard University

  • 1968 - Helen died at the age of 88.Her name and spirit lived on in all the good things that she accomplished.She paved the way for those with disabilities around the world.
